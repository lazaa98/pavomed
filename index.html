FAQ

Quick answers.

Straight answers about Wazuh, SIEM deployment, the managed SOC, and pricing.

What is Wazuh, and why do you use it?+
Wazuh is the world's most widely deployed open-source SIEM and XDR platform. It delivers log analysis, threat detection, file integrity monitoring, vulnerability detection, and compliance reporting — with zero license cost. You get enterprise-grade visibility for the price of expertise and operations only.
Where does the SIEM run — your cloud or ours?+
Your choice. Wazuh can run on-premises on your hardware, in your cloud (AWS, Azure, Hetzner, etc.), or on a VPS we manage for you. Your logs stay where you decide — important for GDPR and data-sovereignty requirements.
What does the managed SOC actually do day-to-day?+
We watch Wazuh alerts around the clock, filter noise, investigate suspicious activity, escalate real threats with clear instructions, keep detection rules tuned, and deliver a monthly plain-language security report. Critical alerts are escalated within 15 minutes.
Do we keep the SIEM if we cancel?+
Yes. The Wazuh deployment is yours — installed in your environment with full documentation and handover. If you stop the managed service, the platform keeps working and your team keeps full access. No lock-in.
Do you also handle firewalls and Fortinet?+
Yes. Hands-on FortiGate expertise — configuration, VPN, IPS profiles, segmentation — and firewall logs are integrated into Wazuh so the SOC sees your network and endpoints in one pipeline. (No official Fortinet partner status claimed.)
How are prices calculated?+
Pricing depends on the number of agents, log sources, retention requirements, number of locations, and response scope. Because Wazuh has no license fees, costs stay predictable as you grow. Listed prices are realistic starting points for SMBs.
How can I get started?+
Book a short scoping call or request a live Wazuh demo. We'll map your log sources, size the deployment, and give you a fixed quote — deployment typically goes live within two weeks.
